Market Analysis and Industry Context

Total addressable market (TAM): Canadian foodservice distribution represents
a $95B annual market. Broadline distribution is ~$62B of that (65%);
specialty distribution is the balance.

Serviceable addressable market (SAM): Our geographic focus (Quebec, Ontario,
Maritimes) represents ~$38B of the TAM. Independent restaurants within that
footprint are ~$14B.

Serviceable obtainable market (SOM): Realistic share ceiling of ~$1.8B
(13% of the independent-restaurant SAM) based on fleet and warehouse
capacity without major expansion.

Industry report references: Technomic 2025 Canadian Foodservice Landscape;
NACS State of the Industry Report; IBISWorld Canadian Food Wholesaling
report (OD4512CA).

Category growth: Canadian foodservice category trend of ~2% CAGR historically;
independent-restaurant subsegment has negative growth of -1.3% in 2024 as
demographic and cost pressures compress margins.

Sector analysis: Three-year outlook shows continued consolidation pressure.
Category-level trend toward direct supplier-to-operator relationships
mediated by AI ordering agents represents the most material structural
headwind facing traditional distributors.

Demographic: End-consumer demand (diners eating at independent restaurants)
expected to remain stable. The question is who supplies those restaurants.
